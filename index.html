Step‑by‑Step Worked Example

Here is the exact five‑step flow used in our sample valuation, so you can see the logic before opening the full walkthrough.

Steps 1–2 · Forecast Cash Flows

Start from last year’s free cash flow (in our example, $50 million) and apply a 5% growth rate to project Years 1–5 (ending around $63.8 million).

Step 3 · Discount Rate & Terminal Value

Choose a required return (we use 10%) and a long‑term growth rate (3%), then estimate a terminal value based on the Year 6 cash flow and the formula TV = CF6 / (r − g).

Steps 4–5 · Present Value & Sensitivity

Discount each yearly cash flow and the terminal value back to today, add them up to get an intrinsic value estimate, and then see how it changes as you vary growth and discount rates.

Core Concepts at a Glance

Cash Flow

Money a business generates after paying operating and capital costs.

Discount Rate

Reflects risk and time value when converting future cash to today’s value.

Growth

Reasonable assumptions about how cash flows may expand over time.

Terminal Value

Value after the forecast period, discounted back to today.

Before You Value — Quick Checklist

  • Understand the business model and revenue drivers.
  • Review recent financials for one‑off items.
  • Choose a cautious base growth and test ranges.
  • Pick a discount rate consistent with risk.
